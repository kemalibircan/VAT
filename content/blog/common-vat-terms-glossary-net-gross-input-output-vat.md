# Common VAT Terms Glossary (Net, Gross, Input/Output VAT…)

VAT talk can sound like a different language. This glossary translates common terms into practical, plain-English definitions you can use when reading invoices and receipts.

This is general information, not tax advice. Rates can change—confirm the correct rate for your case.

## Net (VAT-exclusive)

**Net** is the price before VAT is added.

If you know net and a rate, you can calculate gross:

- `gross = net × (1 + rate/100)`

## Gross (VAT-inclusive)

**Gross** is the price after VAT is included—the amount you typically pay.

To get net from gross:

- `net = gross ÷ (1 + rate/100)`

## VAT amount

The **VAT amount** is the tax slice:

- `VAT = gross − net`

## Input VAT (high-level)

Input VAT is a commonly used term for VAT related to purchases/expenses (in plain English: VAT on what you buy). How it is treated can depend on rules and your setup, so treat this definition as a conceptual starting point.

## Output VAT (high-level)

Output VAT is often used to describe VAT related to sales (VAT on what you charge). Again, exact definitions and reporting rules vary—confirm official guidance for your jurisdiction.

## VAT-inclusive vs VAT-exclusive

- **VAT-inclusive** usually means gross (VAT is already included).
- **VAT-exclusive** usually means net (VAT will be added on top).

## Reverse VAT (remove VAT)

Reverse VAT means taking a VAT-inclusive number and splitting it into net + VAT using:

- `net = gross ÷ (1 + rate/100)`

People often call this “remove VAT” or “VAT inclusive to exclusive”.

## Rounding

Rounding is where real invoices get messy:

- Some systems round per line item.
- Others keep higher precision and round at the end.

If two sources disagree by 0.01, it’s often rounding policy, not bad maths.

## Worked example (illustration only)

Assume an **example** VAT rate of **20%** and a gross price of **60.00**:

1. Factor = 1 + 20/100 = 1.20
2. Net = 60.00 ÷ 1.20 = 50.00
3. VAT = 60.00 − 50.00 = 10.00

So at the example rate, **60.00 gross = 50.00 net + 10.00 VAT**.
