# Do You Charge VAT/GST on Services? (General Guide)

Services are where people get unsure: “Do I add VAT/GST to my day rate?” “Should I quote inclusive or exclusive?” “What does my client expect?”

This guide doesn’t tell you what you *must* do (rules vary). Instead, it gives you a high-level way to think about service pricing and the maths behind net, VAT/GST, and gross. This is general information, not tax advice.

## Start with the pricing question: net or gross?

When you quote a service, you can think in:

- **Net (exclusive)**: your pre-tax price, tax added on top.
- **Gross (inclusive)**: the all-in price the client pays.

Either way, the maths connects them.

## The simple calculation

With a tax rate of `r%`:

- `gross = net × (1 + r/100)`
- `net = gross ÷ (1 + r/100)`
- `tax = gross − net`

## Worked example: turning a net service fee into a gross quote (illustration only)

Assume an **example** rate of **20%** and a net service fee of **500.00**:

1. Factor = 1 + 20/100 = 1.20
2. Gross = 500.00 × 1.20 = 600.00
3. Tax = 600.00 − 500.00 = 100.00

So at the example rate, a 500.00 net service fee becomes 600.00 gross.

## Practical invoice clarity

Even if you quote gross, it’s helpful to show a breakdown on the invoice:

- Net (what the work cost before tax)
- Tax amount (VAT/GST slice)
- Gross (what’s payable)

This reduces back-and-forth and makes approvals easier.
