# VAT Refund: Net vs Gross — What You Actually Get Back?

Refunds can feel confusing because you’re looking at money moving backwards: totals get reversed, discounts might be involved, and receipts don’t always show a clean breakdown.

This guide doesn’t cover refund policy. Instead it explains the maths so you can interpret refund numbers in net/VAT/gross terms. This is general information, not tax advice. Rates can change—confirm the correct rate for your case.

## The key idea: a refund can reverse the same breakdown

If you originally paid a VAT-inclusive (gross) amount, that gross amount can be split into:

- net
- VAT

When you refund the gross, you can reverse the VAT using the same formula you’d use for any gross price.

## The formula (gross → net)

With a rate `r%`:

- `net = gross ÷ (1 + r/100)`
- `VAT = gross − net`

## Worked example: refunding a gross amount (illustration only)

Assume an **example** VAT rate of **20%** and a refund amount of **120.00** (gross).

1. Factor = 1 + 20/100 = 1.20
2. Net = 120.00 ÷ 1.20 = 100.00
3. VAT = 120.00 − 100.00 = 20.00

So at the example rate, a 120.00 gross refund implies 100.00 net and 20.00 VAT.

## Partial refunds and “why the cents change”

Refunds are often partial:

- one item refunded from a multi-item cart
- shipping not refunded
- discount applied across items

That’s where rounding differences show up. If you’re off by 0.01, it’s usually rounding policy (line vs total).
