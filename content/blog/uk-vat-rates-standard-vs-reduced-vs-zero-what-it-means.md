# UK VAT Rates: Standard vs Reduced vs Zero (What It Means)

UK VAT has different **rate categories**. You’ll often hear “standard”, “reduced”, and “zero-rated”. Those labels matter because they change the VAT slice of your invoice.

This is general information, not tax advice. Rates can change and rules can be detailed. Always confirm current guidance for your case.

## The categories in plain English

At a high level:

- **Standard rate**: the “default” VAT category for many goods/services.
- **Reduced rate**: a lower VAT category for some items.
- **Zero-rated**: VAT is treated as 0% for the supply (but it’s still a VAT category).

There are also cases that can be treated differently depending on the context. The point here is the category impacts the maths.

## How the category changes the calculation

The calculator doesn’t need to know the category name. It just needs a rate number.

If your chosen rate is `r%`:

- Add VAT: `gross = net × (1 + r/100)`
- VAT amount: `VAT = gross − net`
- Remove VAT: `net = gross ÷ (1 + r/100)`

## Worked example: comparing two example rates

We’ll use **example** rates to show the effect (not official numbers).

Assume net is **100.00**.

- Example rate A: 20%  
  Gross = `100.00 × 1.20 = 120.00`  
  VAT = `20.00`

- Example rate B: 5%  
  Gross = `100.00 × 1.05 = 105.00`  
  VAT = `5.00`

Same net price, very different VAT slice and gross total.

## Practical takeaway for freelancers

If you are quoting net prices, changing the VAT category changes what your client pays. If you are quoting gross prices, changing the category changes what you keep as net.

So before you copy numbers into an invoice, confirm:

- Which category applies to your supply
- Which percentage rate you should use
