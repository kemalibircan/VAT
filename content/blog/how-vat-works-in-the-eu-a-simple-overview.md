# How VAT Works in the EU: A Simple Overview

EU VAT can look complicated because it involves different countries, different rules, and different rate categories. But the basic *maths* behind VAT is simple: it’s a percentage added to a net price.

This article is a high-level overview for understanding net/gross pricing and checking calculations. It is **general information, not tax advice**. Rates can change, so always confirm the correct rate for your case.

## Net, VAT, gross: the universal building blocks

Most VAT breakdowns use:

- **Net**: price before VAT (exclusive)
- **VAT**: tax slice
- **Gross**: total including VAT (inclusive)

The formulas don’t change by country:

- Add VAT: `gross = net × (1 + rate/100)`
- Remove VAT: `net = gross ÷ (1 + rate/100)`
- VAT amount: `gross − net`

## Why EU VAT feels “different”

VAT feels complex because:

- Rates vary by country.
- Some categories can have different rates.
- Cross-border selling can involve extra concepts and documentation.

But for the purpose of checking a number on an invoice, you usually just need the right rate and the correct direction (add vs remove).

## Worked example (illustration only)

Assume an **example** VAT rate of **21%** and a net price of **100.00**:

1. Factor = `1 + 21/100 = 1.21`
2. Gross = `100.00 × 1.21 = 121.00`
3. VAT = `121.00 − 100.00 = 21.00`

## Practical tips for reading EU-style invoices

- Identify whether the listed prices are net or gross.
- Look for the VAT rate on the invoice line or summary.
- Expect small rounding differences if there are many line items.
