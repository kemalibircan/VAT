# GST in Australia Explained (Simple Examples)

GST in Australia is often discussed in a straightforward way: a percentage is applied to a price, and invoices commonly show the tax slice.

This guide explains net vs gross thinking and gives a couple of easy examples you can recreate with a calculator. It is general information, not tax advice. Rates can change—always confirm the correct rate for your case.

## Net vs gross (GST-exclusive vs GST-inclusive)

You’ll often see:

- **GST-exclusive (net)**: before GST is added
- **GST-inclusive (gross)**: after GST is included

The maths is the same idea as VAT calculators: gross = net + tax.

## The formulas (same structure as VAT)

If the GST rate is `r%`:

- Gross = Net × (1 + r/100)
- Net = Gross ÷ (1 + r/100)
- GST amount = Gross − Net

## Worked example (illustration only)

Let’s use an **example** GST rate of **10%** and net price **200.00**.
This is an illustration rate—always confirm the correct rate for your case.

1. Factor = 1 + 10/100 = 1.10
2. Gross = 200.00 × 1.10 = 220.00
3. GST = 220.00 − 200.00 = 20.00

## Practical uses

- Checking receipts and invoices
- Setting prices when you think in net but need a customer-facing gross number
