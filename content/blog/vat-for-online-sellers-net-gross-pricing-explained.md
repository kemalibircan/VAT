# VAT for Online Sellers: Net/Gross Pricing Explained

Online selling mixes two worlds: customers want simple “what I pay” pricing, while businesses often plan in net amounts and account for VAT separately.

This article explains the net/gross basics and shows how to check your pricing with a calculator. It is general information, not tax advice. Rates can change. Always confirm the correct rate for your case.

## The key question: what does the customer see?

From a customer’s perspective:

- A **VAT-inclusive (gross)** price is straightforward: “That’s what I pay.”

From a seller’s perspective:

- A **VAT-exclusive (net)** price helps you see pre-tax revenue and compare margins.

## The formulas that connect net and gross

If the VAT rate is `r%`:

- Gross = Net × (1 + r/100)
- Net = Gross ÷ (1 + r/100)
- VAT amount = Gross − Net

## Worked example: setting a gross price from a target net (example rate)

Assume you want **50.00 net** and use an **example** VAT rate of **20%**:

1. Factor = 1 + 20/100 = 1.20
2. Gross = 50.00 × 1.20 = 60.00
3. VAT = 60.00 − 50.00 = 10.00

So at the example rate, a 60.00 gross price implies 50.00 net and 10.00 VAT.

## E-commerce gotchas

- **Discounts** can change the taxable base (and therefore VAT).  
- **Shipping** can be treated differently depending on context.  
- **Rounding** can vary between line items and cart totals.

When something looks off, it’s often rounding policy rather than a wrong formula.
