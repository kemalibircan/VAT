# VAT Basics in the UK for Freelancers (Plain English)

If you freelance in the UK, you’ll bump into VAT sooner or later—either on your own invoices or on what your clients and suppliers send you.

This guide is a plain-English overview of the concepts behind VAT and the invoice maths. It’s **general information, not tax advice**. Rates can change, so always confirm the correct rate for your case.

## The three numbers: net, VAT, and gross

Most VAT arithmetic is just these three numbers:

- **Net**: the price before VAT (VAT-exclusive)
- **VAT**: the tax slice
- **Gross**: net + VAT (VAT-inclusive total)

A calculator is useful because it keeps all three visible at the same time.

## The core formulas (add and remove)

If the VAT rate is `r%`:

- Add VAT: `gross = net × (1 + r/100)`
- Remove VAT (reverse): `net = gross ÷ (1 + r/100)`
- VAT amount: `gross − net`

## Worked example (illustration only)

Let’s use an **example** VAT rate of **20%** and a net amount of **100.00**.
This rate is for illustration only—always confirm the correct rate for your case.

1. Factor = `1 + 20/100 = 1.20`
2. Gross = `100.00 × 1.20 = 120.00`
3. VAT = `120.00 − 100.00 = 20.00`

## What to look for on UK-style invoices

Many invoices include:

- The VAT rate (sometimes more than one if items differ)
- Net amount
- VAT amount
- Gross total

If anything feels “off”, check whether the price you’re starting from is net or gross—mixing those up causes most mistakes.
