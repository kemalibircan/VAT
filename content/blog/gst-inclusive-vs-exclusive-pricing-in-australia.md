# GST Inclusive vs Exclusive Pricing in Australia

If you sell or buy in Australia, you’ll often see pricing described as “GST-inclusive” or “GST-exclusive”. It’s the same idea as VAT-inclusive vs VAT-exclusive: the label tells you whether the tax is already included.

This guide explains the concept and gives a quick worked example. It is general information, not tax advice. Rates can change—always confirm the correct rate for your case.

## Inclusive vs exclusive in one breath

- **GST-exclusive (net)**: price before GST is added  
- **GST-inclusive (gross)**: price after GST is included

## The formulas

If the GST rate is `r%`:

- `gross = net × (1 + r/100)`
- `net = gross ÷ (1 + r/100)`
- `GST = gross − net`

## Worked example (illustration only)

Assume an **example** GST rate of **10%** and a net price of **80.00**:

1. Factor = 1 + 10/100 = 1.10
2. Gross = 80.00 × 1.10 = 88.00
3. GST = 88.00 − 80.00 = 8.00

So at the example rate, **80.00 net becomes 88.00 gross**.

## Why it matters in day-to-day work

- You compare quotes correctly (apples-to-apples).
- You understand what a “$100 budget” means (net or gross).
- You can spot when GST was applied twice by mistake.
