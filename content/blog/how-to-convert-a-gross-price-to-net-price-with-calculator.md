# How to Convert a Gross Price to Net Price (With Calculator)

You have a VAT/GST-inclusive price (gross) and need the tax-exclusive amount (net). This is one of the most common “reverse VAT” use cases—especially for receipts, supplier prices, and “all-in budgets”.

This guide shows the formula, a worked example (with an example rate), and how to recreate it with a calculator.

## The gross → net formula

If the tax rate is `r%`:

- `net = gross ÷ (1 + r/100)`
- `tax = gross − net`

## Worked example (illustration only)

Assume an **example** rate of **20%** and a gross amount of **240.00**.

1. Factor = `1 + 20/100 = 1.20`
2. Net = `240.00 ÷ 1.20 = 200.00`
3. Tax = `240.00 − 200.00 = 40.00`

So at the example rate, **240.00 gross = 200.00 net + 40.00 tax**.

## Quick checks before you trust the result

- Confirm the price you have is genuinely gross (inclusive).
- Confirm the rate you used applies to your case.
- If you’re off by 0.01, it’s probably rounding policy.
