# VAT on Digital Products: What Changes? (High-level)

Digital products can make VAT discussions feel more complicated: the product is “intangible”, customers can be in different places, and platforms may handle parts of the tax process.

This guide stays high-level and focuses on the pricing maths (net/VAT/gross) that you can control and sanity-check. It is general information, not tax advice. Rates can change—confirm the correct rate for your case.

## What usually changes (conceptually)

Without going into legal detail, the topics that often come up are:

- Where the customer is located
- How you classify the product or service
- Whether a marketplace/platform is involved

Those questions affect which rate might apply and who is responsible for collecting it.

## What doesn’t change: the maths

Once you have a rate, the calculations are still:

- `gross = net × (1 + rate/100)`
- `net = gross ÷ (1 + rate/100)`
- `VAT = gross − net`

## Worked example: net to gross (illustration only)

Assume an **example** rate of **20%** and a net price of **25.00**:

1. Factor = 1 + 20/100 = 1.20
2. Gross = 25.00 × 1.20 = 30.00
3. VAT = 30.00 − 25.00 = 5.00

## Practical tip: be consistent about what you display

If you show gross pricing to customers, make sure your internal reporting still tracks net and VAT separately so you can reconcile payouts and invoices.
